## What Are Mutual Funds?

A **mutual fund** is a pooled investment vehicle where many investors combine their money, which is then managed by professional fund managers who invest it across a diversified portfolio of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.).

Think of it like a group of people pooling money to hire a chef and buy ingredients together — everyone gets a share of the meal proportional to what they contributed.

---

## How They Work

1. **You invest money** → buy "units" of the fund at the current NAV (Net Asset Value)
2. **Fund manager pools money** from thousands of investors
3. **Manager invests** across many securities based on the fund's objective
4. **Returns are distributed** proportionally to unit holders
5. **You can redeem** units at any time (for open-ended funds) at the prevailing NAV

---

## Key Concepts

**NAV (Net Asset Value)** — the per-unit price of the fund, calculated daily as total assets ÷ total units.

**Expense Ratio** — annual fee charged by the fund (e.g., 0.5–2%), deducted from returns.

**SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)** — investing a fixed amount monthly, which averages out market volatility over time.

---

## Types of Mutual Funds

| Type | Invests In | Risk Level | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Equity Funds** | Stocks | High | Long-term wealth creation |
| **Debt Funds** | Bonds, govt securities | Low–Medium | Stable returns, short-term |
| **Hybrid Funds** | Mix of equity + debt | Medium | Balanced growth |
| **Index Funds** | Mirrors a market index (Nifty 50, Sensex) | Market-level | Passive, low-cost investing |
| **ELSS** | Equity (tax-saving) | High | Tax saving under Section 80C |
| **Liquid Funds** | Very short-term debt | Very Low | Parking idle cash |

---

## Advantages

- **Diversification** — spreads risk across many assets
- **Professional management** — experts handle investment decisions
- **Affordable** — start with as little as ₹500/month via SIP
- **Liquidity** — easy to buy/sell (open-ended funds)
- **Regulated** — governed by SEBI in India, providing investor protection
- **Transparent** — NAV published daily, portfolios disclosed monthly

## Disadvantages

- **Not risk-free** — market-linked funds can lose value
- **Fees** — expense ratios eat into returns
- **No control** — you can't choose individual stocks
- **Tax implications** — gains are taxable depending on type and holding period

---

## Tax Treatment in India

- **Equity funds held >1 year** → Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) taxed at 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh
- **Equity funds held <1 year** → Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) taxed at 20%
- **Debt funds** → Taxed as per your income slab

Mutual funds suit almost everyone — from beginners wanting simple diversification to experienced investors seeking specific exposures. The key is matching the **fund type to your goal, time horizon, and risk tolerance**.
